# Sample dynamics

This note records how the panel-dynamics tables should be read in the paper. The tables are generated by `scripts/prepare_analysis_panel.py` for each output scope.

## Generated tables

Each scope directory under `outputs/analysis_panel/` includes:

- `analysis_panel_balance_by_institution.csv`: one row per `UNITID`, with first year, last year, observed years, missing years, sector at first and last observation, and full-window balance.
- `analysis_panel_balance_summary.csv`: institution counts, balanced-panel share, mean observed years, first-observed-after-start counts, last-observed-before-end counts, and sector-change counts.
- `analysis_entry_exit_by_sector_year.csv`: first and last observed counts by year and sector. These are window-based measures, not verified openings or closures.
- `analysis_entry_exit_reason_audit.csv`: one row for each sample entry or sample exit, classified by whether the `UNITID` first appears in the full panel, disappears from the full panel, changes `PSET4FLG`, changes `SECTOR`, or changes both.
- `analysis_institution_years_by_sector_year.csv`: institution-years and distinct institutions by sector and year.
- `analysis_min_years_sensitivity.csv`: institutions and institution-years retained when requiring at least 1 through 15 observed years.

## Definitions

The analysis window is 2009 through 2023. A balanced institution is observed in all 15 years in the relevant sample scope.

The entry and exit table uses first and last observed years inside the research window. A first observation after 2009 can reflect true entry, a change in Title IV or four-year status, a sector change, or reporting coverage. A last observation before 2023 can reflect closure, merger, loss of Title IV eligibility, sector change, or no longer satisfying the four-year sample rule.

Those tables should therefore be described as sample entry and sample exit unless a later institution-level check verifies actual openings, closures, or mergers.

The reason audit compares the sample event row with the nearest full-panel row outside the sample spell. For sample entry, the comparison is the nearest prior full-panel row for the same `UNITID`. For sample exit, the comparison is the nearest later full-panel row. The categories are:

- `full_panel_first_appearance`: the institution has no earlier row in the full panel during the analysis window.
- `full_panel_disappearance`: the institution has no later row in the full panel during the analysis window.
- `pset4flg_transition`: the institution remains in the full panel, but `PSET4FLG` changes across the sample boundary.
- `sector_transition`: the institution remains in the full panel, but `SECTOR` changes across the sample boundary.
- `pset4flg_and_sector_transition`: both `PSET4FLG` and `SECTOR` change across the sample boundary.
- `full_panel_gap_or_other`: the institution appears on both sides of the sample boundary, but the nearest observed rows do not show a `PSET4FLG` or `SECTOR` change. This should be rare and should be inspected before being described in the paper.

## External NCES context

The paper should separate this study's sample mechanics from national changes in the IPEDS universe.

NCES describes IPEDS as an annual institution-level system covering postsecondary providers, with Institutional Characteristics determining control, level, location, and related fields. The Cost component collects student charges and cost-of-attendance values used by financial-aid offices. The Student Financial Aid component collects grant and loan counts and amounts, and the Finance component uses separate reporting forms for public, private nonprofit, and private for-profit institutions.

Sources:

- NCES, IPEDS Survey Methodology: https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/survey-components/ipeds-survey-methodology
- NCES, IPEDS Table 1, Title IV institutions by control, level, and region, 2021-22: https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/Search/ViewTable?tableId=32461
- NCES Digest of Education Statistics, Table 317.10, degree-granting institutions by control and level, 1949-50 through 2022-23: https://nces.ed.gov/programs/digest/d23/tables/dt23_317.10.asp

Digest Table 317.10 shows a national decline in degree-granting institution counts after the early 2010s. The national table is broader than this paper's baseline because it includes two-year and four-year institutions and all controls. NCES also notes that institution counts over time can be affected by whether branch campuses report separately.

## Paper wording

Use wording like this in the data section:

> The panel is intentionally unbalanced. Institutions enter and leave the research sample as they appear in the clean IPEDS panel and satisfy the four-year Title IV sector rule. I therefore report first and last observed years within the sample window, not verified openings or closures. In the baseline public and private nonprofit sample, 1,959 of 2,774 institutions are observed in all 15 years from 2009 through 2023. The combined baseline rises through 2018 and then declines modestly, while the sector files differ: public institutions grow through 2023, and private nonprofit institutions decline after their 2015 peak.

For the national context:

> National NCES Digest counts show a broader post-early-2010s decline in degree-granting institutions, especially outside the paper's baseline sector scope. I treat that pattern as context, not as a direct sample restriction. The empirical tables therefore report balance, first and last observation, and minimum-years sensitivity within the exact baseline and sector-specific samples used for estimation.
